VS Series
Money is Media

For my final thesis at MCAD I created a set of four projects exploring the relationships between data, money, and media. The first project, Recursion vs Consumption, was a data visualization of months of my checking account activity using forks stuck in a wall. The visualization was plotted across the wall over time, each fork represented 3 dollars and each withdrawal removed forks from the wall while deposits added forks.

The second project, Module vs Interval, was an attempt to collect and document one million dollars of my own money. It involved withdrawing one thousand dollars at a time taking it home and documenting it and then redepositing it back into my account. Being a student, I barely had $1500 in my account so withdrawing $1000 at a time was the most I could hope for. I never reached one million, but I did document around $60,000 worth of my own money.

The third and fourth projects were more abstract and performance based, they also lack proper documentation. In one project, I read aloud from a bible while I whited out the words. I forget exactly what my argument was there. Something about making words have a finite use. Also it was a bible, so there. The last project was a tiny projection of my active credit card number in a public space. But as someone attempted to get close and read it, it would get even smaller until it was illegible.
