I received my BFA from The Minneapolis College of Art and Design in 2006. Along with my interactive media coursework, I took the standard art school foundation classes for design, illustration, photography, sculpture, and film. I also took more specialized courses in printmaking, motion graphics, typography, and kinetic art.

Also during my time, I won a merit scholarship for my portfolio of interactive work. The main interface for the portfolio was a photograph of a fish on a cutting board that would get chopped each time you viewed a piece of work until the fish was just a bloody mess. One of my instructors Piotr Szyhalski described it as "cute". It's the greatest compliment I received in all of my work at MCAD.
